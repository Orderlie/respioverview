Upper Respiratory Tract

Conducting Zone Warms & Filters

The Nose

  • Divided by the septum. Contains 3 passageways called turbinates (conchae).
  • Lined with highly vascular, ciliated nasal mucosa.
  • Core Function: Filter, humidify, and warm inspired air.

Paranasal Sinuses

  • 4 pairs: frontal, ethmoid, sphenoid, maxillary.
  • Function as a resonating chamber for speech.
  • Clinical Note: Because they are continuous with nasal mucosa, they are a common site of infection.

Larynx (Voice Box)

  • Epiglottis: Flap that closes over trachea during swallowing.
  • Glottis: Opening between vocal cords.
  • Functions include voice production and coughing.

The Watchdog

The larynx is often called the "watchdog of the lungs" because it protects the lower airway from aspiration.

Lower Respiratory Tract

Respiratory Zone Gas Exchange

Trachea & Bronchi

  • Trachea contains C-shaped cartilage rings to prevent collapse.
  • Bifurcates into R & L main bronchi. The right bronchus is wider and straighter (higher risk of aspiration).
  • Bronchioles have no cartilage; patency relies on elastic recoil.

Lungs & Pleura

  • Right lung: 3 lobes. Left lung: 2 lobes.
  • Visceral pleura covers the lungs.
  • Parietal pleura lines the thoracic cavity.

The Alveoli (The Magic Happens Here)

  • ~300 million alveoli providing massive surface area for O₂–CO₂ exchange.
  • Type I cells (95%): Barrier structure.
  • Type II cells (5%): Produce surfactant to reduce surface tension and prevent collapse.
  • Alveolar macrophages provide phagocytic defense.

The Breathing Cycle

Inspiration

An active process. Diaphragm and intercostals contract, thoracic cavity expands, pressure drops, air rushes in.

Expiration

Normally a passive process. Lungs and chest wall recoil, cavity shrinks, pressure rises, air forced out.

Compliance

The measure of the elasticity and expandability of the lungs.

Decreased Compliance = "Stiff Lungs"
Seen in conditions like pulmonary fibrosis, ARDS, and pulmonary edema.

Airway Resistance

Determined by the radius of the airway. Increased by:

  • Smooth muscle contraction (Asthma)
  • Thickening mucosa (Chronic bronchitis)
  • Loss of elasticity (Emphysema)
